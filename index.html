Contents :: The airframe

Corrugate, considered.

The BBD-1 begins as a die-cut sheet of EB-flute corrugate sealed in a bio-resin weather coat. Fold along the scores, click in the avionics puck and four motor pods, and the shipping box becomes the aircraft. When the mission ends, the electronics unclip and the airframe goes back into the recycling stream it came from.

Material

Weather-treated corrugate

The coating shrugs off rain, humidity, and 40 knot gusts. Rated IPX4, from minus 10 to plus 45 Celsius, without giving up the weight advantage of paper.

Logistics

Flat-pack by design

1,000 airframes travel on a single pallet. Print them like boxes, stage them like inventory, deploy them like software.

End of life

Recyclable in seconds

Motors, battery, and avionics pop out by hand. Everything left over is certified for the standard corrugate recycling stream.

Handling :: Fully autonomous

Ten thousand aircraft. Zero pilots.

Every BBD-1 carries the full autonomy stack onboard: perception, planning, and control. Units share position and intent over an encrypted mesh, negotiate airspace among themselves, and close around failures in milliseconds.

  • Onboard everything. Each unit flies itself even when the network does not exist.
  • Mesh consensus. Deconfliction and routing are negotiated peer to peer, in flight.
  • Graceful loss. Lose one node, or a hundred, and the swarm closes around the gap.

Routing :: GPS optional

When GPS goes dark, we plug in.

BBD-1 navigates on vision and inertia first, satellites second. For tunnels, dense urban cores, warehouses, and disaster zones, an optional spool pays out a hair-thin fiber-optic line as the aircraft flies. Hard-wired guidance with zero radio dependence, immune to jamming and spoofing by construction.
